## 2. Professional Sketch Layout Patterns

When generating diagrams or sketchnotes, use these structured layout frameworks to arrange components logically:

### Iceberg Layout (Tảng Băng Chìm)
- **Use for**: Illustrating visible outcomes vs hidden foundation/efforts (e.g., success vs hard work, frontend vs backend).
- **Structure**: An iceberg floating in water. 10% visible above water (labeled with surface outcomes), 90% massive shape submerged under water (labeled with system processes, operations, and hard work).

### Growth Flywheel Layout (Bánh Đà Tăng Trưởng)
- **Use for**: Self-reinforcing loops, compounding growth, or automated business processes.
- **Structure**: A large central circular gear/wheel with 3-4 curved arrows pointing sequentially around the circumference in a continuous clockwise circle. Each stage feeds directly into the next.

### Journey/Staircase Layout (Hành Trình / Bậc Thang)
- **Use for**: Roadmap planning, step-by-step career growth, or user journey maps.
- **Structure**: A winding path climbing upwards toward a mountain peak, or a flight of stairs ascending from left to right. Key milestones are marked with small flags, signposts, or numbered steps.

### Conversion Funnel Layout (Phễu Chuyển Đổi)
- **Use for**: Marketing conversions, lead filtering, or step-by-step information filtering.
- **Structure**: A wide-top funnel divided into 3-4 horizontal layers (Top: Awareness, Middle: Interest/Consideration, Bottom: Action/Conversion), showing inputs filtering down into a concentrated output.

### Balance Scale Layout (Cán Cân Đối Trọng)
- **Use for**: Side-by-side comparisons, pros vs cons, cost vs benefit, manual vs automated.
- **Structure**: A classic two-pan mechanical scale. The two sides balance or tip slightly, with labels placed inside or next to each scale pan to represent opposing values.
